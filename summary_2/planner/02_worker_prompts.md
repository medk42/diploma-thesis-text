# Worker Prompts

## Discussion Research Worker
Use this when rereading the thesis for Chapter 6 material:

```text
You are extracting source material for a richer Chapter 6 discussion.

Focus on:
- what the thesis built,
- what the results mean,
- why they matter,
- where the system is useful,
- where it is not useful.

Separate your output into:
1. grounded thesis claims,
2. safe authorial interpretation,
3. claims that must be softened heavily.

Pay special attention to:
- pick-and-place as a positive flexibility result,
- welding as the strongest practical fit,
- the modular architecture as a real result,
- the system as a human-in-the-loop workflow,
- the thesis as a platform for repeated task packages rather than a universal robot language.

Do not overclaim speed, precision, production readiness, or general industrial superiority.
```

## Limitations And Future Work Worker
Use this when rereading the thesis for the Chapter 6 limitation section and Chapter 7 future work:

```text
You are extracting source material for limitations and future work.

Rank limitations by practical importance:
- what threatens the system's usefulness,
- what is a real conceptual limit,
- what is mostly engineering debt or UI polish,
- what is future work but not a thesis result.

Check specifically:
- robot-side export / compilation,
- scene understanding as the biggest limit,
- planning and collision avoidance,
- tool abstraction,
- Linux and cross-platform status,
- hand tracking / VR / voice,
- teach-pendant integration,
- evaluation and precision characterization.

Mark every item as T, I, or Author-view.
```

## Style Worker
Use this when drafting text, not just notes:

```text
Write in the style of the existing thesis:
- direct technical register,
- explicit mechanism -> rationale -> consequence,
- restrained tone,
- no sales language,
- no generic AI-summary phrasing.

Keep Chapter 6 rooted in the introduction and keep Chapter 7 concise.
```
