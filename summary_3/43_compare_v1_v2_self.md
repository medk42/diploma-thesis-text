# Self comparison: current chapters vs v2

## Bottom line

The `v2` pair is better and should replace the current chapters if you want the stronger presentation. The change is not a major rewrite of thesis claims. It is a targeted structural improvement that makes the positive results easier to retain, makes the evaluation boundary more deliberate, and gives formal evaluation more appropriate prominence in future work.

## Where v2 is better

### Chapter 6

- `v2` adds an explicit `What worked well` section. This fixes the main remaining tonal imbalance in the current chapter, where strong results are present but are surrounded more tightly by caveats.
- `v2` separates `Current limitations` from `Evaluation boundary`. This improves the chapter structure because the qualitative evaluation now reads as a deliberate thesis-level boundary, not just another limitation bullet.
- The new positive section is thesis-grounded rather than inflated. It names task-oriented authoring, modular architecture, reusable use-case boundary, and end-to-end workflow, all of which are already strongly supported by Chapters 2 to 5.

### Chapter 7

- `v2` makes the chapter hierarchy clearer by separating strongest conceptual result from strongest practical result.
- `v2` promotes formal evaluation to the first near-term future-work item. This better matches the actual state of the thesis, where architecture and workflow are demonstrated but formal evidence is still missing.
- The execution-future-work paragraph is slightly stronger in `v2` because it now also mentions clearer capability reporting at the robot interface boundary, which fits the architecture chapters well.

## What stayed appropriately conservative

- `v2` still keeps welding as the strongest practical fit and keeps pick-and-place as more of an architectural demonstration than a proof of broad practical readiness.
- `v2` does not change the prototype boundary into a stronger deployment claim.
- `v2` does not invent quantitative evidence, user-study results, or broader industrial validation.

## Possible downsides

- `v2` is slightly longer, especially in Chapter 6. If the goal were maximal brevity, the current version is leaner.
- The new `What worked well` section adds some repetition with earlier positive statements in Chapter 6, although in my judgment the extra signaling is worth it.
- Because the v2 files are separate copies, they introduce temporary file duplication until one version is chosen.

## Recommendation

Adopt `v2` with at most minor wording polish. The changes improve chapter signaling and chapter hierarchy without changing the core argument or making the thesis sound less honest.
