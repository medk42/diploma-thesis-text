# Self A/B Comparison: Original vs v2 Ending Chapters

## Bottom line

`v2` is the stronger pair and should replace the originals.

## Where v2 is better

The main improvement is voice. The original chapters contained repeated reviewer-style and meta-commentary phrasing such as:

- explaining what the chapter is trying to do
- defending the claims as `scoped but defensible`
- telling the reader how the thesis should be interpreted
- using opponent-like wording such as `the most defensible answer`

The revised `v2` chapters keep the same substantive claims, but state them more directly as thesis conclusions.

`v2` also integrates better with the rest of the thesis. It still keeps the same core hierarchy:

- end-to-end prototype system
- modular runtime with explicit interfaces
- reusable use-case boundary and workflow
- welding as the clearest practical fit
- pick-and-place as the more architecture-demonstrating use case
- qualitative, prototype-level validation only

That hierarchy matches the introduction and the architecture / implementation chapters more naturally than the original phrasing.

The validation boundary is also clearer in `v2`. The revised text now states plainly that:

- the system was demonstrated on a real Kassow robot
- most development and testing took place in simulation
- physical validation remained limited
- the thesis does not claim measured superiority or production readiness

This is more precise and less easy to misread than the original ending.

## Tradeoffs or regressions

The main tradeoff is that `v2` is still somewhat more explicit and interpretive than the originals. The original pair was leaner in places. However, that brevity came with a cost: the prose often sounded like an evaluator describing the thesis from outside.

There is still some overlap between the revised discussion and conclusion, but it is acceptable and materially less problematic than the voice issues in the originals.

## Recommendation

Replace the original ending chapters with `v2`.

The revised pair improves thesis voice, clarifies the evidence boundary, and stays honest about prototype scope without changing the underlying claims. The differences are mostly rhetorical and organizational, but they are important because they make the ending sound like the thesis author concluding the work rather than a reviewer assessing it.
