# Discussion Logic Review

## Strengths
- The contribution hierarchy is correct: modular runtime first, demonstrated task behavior second.
- Pick-and-place is preserved as a positive scene-adaptive result instead of being collapsed into a limitation only.
- Welding is preserved as the stronger trajectory-centric fit without forcing the text to overclaim practical superiority.
- The discussion bank separates strengths, limitations, threats to validity, and claims to avoid cleanly enough for chapter drafting.

## Boundary issues
- The future-work section in the master bank still reads slightly more certain than the source text for evaluation and multimodal-input directions. Those items should stay inferential.
- The filtered Chapter 6 and Chapter 7 files are concise, but they risk losing the raw-source grounding that makes the master file safe.
- The current Chapter 6 bank does not yet foreground save/load conditionality as strongly as it could; that is a useful boundary because it shows why pick-and-place and weld differ in reuse semantics.

## Missing discussion angles
- Mention more explicitly that pick-and-place and weld differ not only in task class but also in when the command becomes fixed: weld at confirmation, pick-and-place partly at execution.
- Mention more explicitly that the UI burden belongs mostly to prototype orchestration maturity, not to the core spatial-authoring concept.
- Keep the motion-primitive use cases as architectural evidence only, not as equal-weight evaluation results.

## Recommended chapter-scope changes
- In Chapter 6, keep `What the thesis achieved` short and let `Limitations and threats to validity` be the longest section.
- In Chapter 6, keep `Claims to avoid` as an internal drafting guardrail; in the final prose, absorb it into the tone of Sections 6.2 and 6.3 instead of making it a visible standalone section unless needed.
- In Chapter 7, keep near-term engineering items separate from longer-term research directions. That split already works and should remain.
- In Chapter 7, the most defensible final takeaway is `workable foundation plus clear next steps`, not `general replacement for existing robot-programming workflows`.
