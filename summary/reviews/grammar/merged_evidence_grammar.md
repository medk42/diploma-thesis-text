# Grammar and Style Review

## Findings
- Medium: [summary/extraction/91_discussion_evidence.md] Section `6.1 What the thesis achieved` uses terse bullets without source reminders, while the master bank uses fuller wording. The style shift is acceptable for a filtered bank, but it makes Chapter 6 feel more skeletal than Chapter 7.
- Medium: [summary/extraction/92_conclusion_evidence.md] Section `7.1 Conclusion` uses `programming-friction`, which reads awkwardly in prose. `programming friction` is cleaner.
- Medium: [summary/extraction/90_master_evidence.md] `Strong Outcomes` mixes `proof`, `result`, `case`, and `fit` for similar strength claims. Normalizing these terms would help later drafting.
- Low: [summary/extraction/90_master_evidence.md] `Keep-Separate or Weakly Grounded Items` is stylistically fine, but `business/usefulness claims` is too colloquial for thesis drafting notes.
- Low: [summary/extraction/91_discussion_evidence.md] `6.2 Where the approach works well` uses both `trajectory-centric result` and `trajectory-authoring proof`. Pick one form.
- Low: [summary/extraction/92_conclusion_evidence.md] `Conclusion Boundary Notes` is useful, but its style is closer to editorial instruction than evidence. That is fine for an internal draft, but the final prose should absorb it rather than mirror it.

## Suggested rewrites
- Replace `programming-friction` with `programming friction`.
- Normalize to `scene-adaptive execution` for pick-and-place and `trajectory-centric spatial authoring` for welding.
- Prefer `main contribution`, `supporting result`, and `limitation` over a mix of `proof`, `case`, and `fit`.
- Replace `business/usefulness claims` with `broader practical-value claims`.

## Consistency notes
- Keep `use case` as two words everywhere.
- Keep `control plane` as two words everywhere.
- Use `task-oriented` consistently when contrasting the system against a full programming language.
- Use `scene-adaptive execution` consistently for pick-and-place.
- Use `trajectory-centric spatial authoring` consistently for seam welding.
